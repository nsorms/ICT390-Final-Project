RAM (Random Access Memory)

RAM is volitile storage, to allow the computer to store open programs and tasks there for temporary holding until it is cleared from memory by the user or power-loss.

Hard Drive/Storage

This is where all of your data will be stored for your computer. The processor will access it and read the correct the data off of it. There are 2 main types of Hard drives: SSDs (Solid State Drive) and HDD (Hard Drive). SSDs have no moving parts, and therefor much faster than traditional HDDs. HDDs have spinning magnetic disks and a read/write head that moves across the disk. With physical moving parts, this style of hard drive is much slower in comparison to SSDs. They are limited essentially by the speed at which the disks can move.

Video Card

A video/graphics card is a special piece of hardware that renders images very quickly for video playback, video games, and certain process intensive tasks that benefit from a GPU (Graphical Processing Unit).
